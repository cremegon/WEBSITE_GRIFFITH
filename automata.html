NieR; Automata

The bold beginning of NieR;Automata starts with a
prompt asking the player a series of questions.

"Do you believe in God?",
"Do you believe there is meaning in this world?",
"Do you think video-games are silly little things?"

In an entertaining but perplexing fashion you finish the short prompt, from which point, Automata releases its plethora of existentialist philosophy in both waves and crumbs that are to be saught out.

It balances itself on the fine line of nebulously dreadful, hopeful, comical and tragic; A combination of all of Taro's past experience
in video-game narrative combined.

Each ending brings you a new perspective into the actions you took previously. Enemies becoming allies, allies becoming enemies, connections made and severed beautifully and brutally, until the rug is pulled and Automata reveal its truth and true beginning, spiralling the player's psychology down the path of ultimately realizing the question:

"What does it mean, to be human?"
